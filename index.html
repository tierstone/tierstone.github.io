Organization

The Tierstone Foundation is a collective of engineers dedicated to the development of principled open-source software. Originally established as TuxForge, the organization was restructured to prioritize architectural integrity, maintainable codebases, and long-term sustainability.

Our mission is to advance open-source development through rigorous engineering standards. We emphasize correctness, clarity, and resilience in every component we produce, ensuring that our contributions remain reliable foundations for future innovation.

Flagship Initiative

Platinum (/p/OS)

Repository

System Overview

Platinum is a 64-bit operating system developed from first principles in strict C99/C11. The system targets the QEMU x86_64 platform with a focus on minimalism, structural clarity, and adherence to modern systems programming practices. Development is conducted under the GPLv3 license.

GPLv3 C99/C11 Strict x86_64

Artificial Intelligence Policy

The use of local open-source language models is permitted solely for syntactic reference and boilerplate generation. All contributions must undergo comprehensive human review. Submissions containing unverified AI-generated code will not be accepted.

Engineering integrity requires deliberate understanding of every component. Automated generation without human comprehension does not constitute valid contribution.
